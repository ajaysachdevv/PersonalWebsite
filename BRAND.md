## 0. How to Use This Document

This is the single source of truth for everything that carries Ajay Sachdev's name. Hand this to any designer, developer, copywriter, or AI assistant and they should be able to produce something on-brand without asking questions.

**Scope:** Personal website, LinkedIn presence, project demos, pitch decks, any future venture under the Ajay Sachdev name. Sub-brands (Uni-Nation, Infinite Ink) have their own accent colours but inherit the core system.

**When in doubt:** Does it feel like a smart friend explaining something over coffee? If yes, it's on-brand. If it sounds like a corporate deck or a ChatGPT cover letter, rewrite it.

## 1. Brand Foundation

### Manifesto

I grew up in a Sindhi family in Mumbai. My dad ran a textile business, so I learned about margins and supplier negotiations before anyone taught me what a spreadsheet was. Studied Financial Markets at Jai Hind College, got my Chartered Wealth Management cert, then went back to the family business and pushed us into garment manufacturing.

Moved to the UK for an MSc in Finance at Loughborough. Now CIMA part-qualified. Joined a B2B SaaS scale-up as an intern and somehow ended up running all of it: management accounts, VAT returns, payroll, compliance, onboarding, across three countries. Seven bank accounts, three currencies, one person.

Somewhere along the way I started building AI systems to solve the problems my job threw at me. Not because it's trendy. Because the tools I needed didn't exist yet.

I sit between departments. The messy stuff nobody owns. That's where I'm happiest.

### Mission

Build things that solve real problems, then show the work honestly.

### Vision

Be known as someone who builds useful things across disciplines — not someone who talks about building things.

### Core Values

| Value | Means | Doesn't mean |
|-------|-------|--------------|
| **Honest** | Say what's real. "Currently paper trading" not "algorithmic trading system." Admit limitations. | Being self-deprecating or underselling |
| **Resourceful** | Learn what's needed. Draft contracts without a law degree. Build AI without a CS degree. | Pretending expertise you don't have |
| **Direct** | Get to the point. Lead with the problem. Kill filler words. | Being blunt or rude |
| **Curious** | Pick up Python through vibe-coding. Explore new domains. Follow the interesting thread. | Being academic or theoretical |
| **Grounded** | Family textile business since 1988. School governor. Real numbers, real results. | Being boring or nostalgic |

### Positioning Statement

For people who hire or collaborate with multi-disciplinary operators, Ajay Sachdev is a finance and operations professional who also builds production AI systems, because he learned that the most interesting problems sit between departments.

### What Makes This Different

Most finance people don't code. Most coders don't understand business operations. Ajay does both, and every project on the site proves it with real code and real numbers.

## 2. Brand Personality & Voice

### Personality Traits

- **Direct, not blunt.** Say it clearly. Don't waste words. But don't be cold.
- **Curious, not academic.** Interested in how things work. Not interested in sounding clever about it.
- **Confident, not arrogant.** The work speaks. Don't add adjectives to make it louder.
- **Casual, not sloppy.** First person. Contractions. Sentence fragments sometimes. But precise when precision matters.
- **Honest, not modest.** "I built a nine-stage pipeline" is honest. "I humbly created a small tool" is false modesty.

### Tone of Voice Principles

**1. Start with the problem, not the solution.**
- Bad: "I built an AI agent that integrates with Teams, Xero, and HubSpot."
- Good: "Nobody could find anything in 7,000 files. So I built a system that searches them in seconds."

**2. Origin stories, not feature lists.**
- Bad: "Multi-agent algorithmic trading system with EMA, VWAP, and RSI indicators."
- Good: "I kept making emotional trades. So I built a system that removes me from the equation."

**3. Specific beats impressive.**
- Bad: "Significant cost savings across the organisation."
- Good: "Renegotiated WiFi (−50%), electricity (−74%), software (−30%+)."

**4. Read it aloud. If it sounds like ChatGPT, rewrite it.**

### Vocabulary

**Never use (inflated verbs):**
architected, spearheaded, orchestrated, engineered, leveraged, synergised, cutting-edge, passionate about, driven individual, thought leader, disruptive, innovative (without evidence), best-in-class, holistic, stakeholder alignment

**Use instead:**
built, made, wrote, set up, fixed, figured out, learned, ran, managed, reduced, improved, created, configured, taught myself

**Never use (employment sensitivity):**
open to new roles, looking for opportunities, job seeking, available for hire, on the market, exploring options, hire me

**Use instead:**
Let's talk, get in touch, always interested in hard problems, builder, maker

### Messaging Hierarchy

**If you have 5 seconds:** Finance & ops by day. Vibe-coding AI by night.

**If you have 30 seconds:** I run finance and operations at a tech scale-up — three countries, seven bank accounts, one person. Nights and weekends I build AI systems. Not because it's trendy, but because the tools I needed didn't exist. My Teams bot searches 7,000 documents. My trading bot removes emotion from the equation.

**If you have 2 minutes:** Add the origin stories. The textile factory in Mumbai. Learning to code through vibe-coding. Drafting contracts without a law degree. The messy cross-department problems nobody owns.

### Platform Voice Adjustments

| Platform | Register | Notes |
|----------|----------|-------|
| Website | Casual-sharp, first person | Full origin stories. Most relaxed register. |
| LinkedIn | Slightly more structured, still first person | Can reference projects but keep it conversational. Never corporate. |
| Email | Warm and direct | Short paragraphs. Get to the point in line one. |
| Pitch deck | Numbers-forward, confident | Lead with metrics. Origin stories in speaker notes. |
| Code README | Technical and practical | No marketing language. Just what it does and how to run it. |

## 3. Employment Sensitivity (Critical)

Ajay's current employer (Krank) does **not** know he is exploring new opportunities. Every piece of communication must pass this filter:

- Would this embarrass Ajay if his COO saw it? If yes, rewrite.
- Position as a **builder** who makes interesting things. Not someone looking for a job.
- The site should make people want to reach out — not broadcast availability.
- Footer CTA: "Let's talk" — never "Hire me."
- Hero badge signals expertise and building, not availability.

## 4. Content Rules

- **Title:** Always "Finance & Operations Manager" — never "Financial Controller"
- **Qualification:** CIMA ongoing, expected May 2026 — never ACCA
- **All claims must be defensible** with facts from `master_cv.md`

## 5. Logo System

### Primary Mark

The brand mark is a geometric **"A"** set in a rounded square. It appears as the favicon and can be used as an avatar across platforms.

**Construction:**
- 32×32 base grid, 6px corner radius on container
- Letter "A" centred, set in the display typeface (Space Grotesk 700)
- Primary version: white letter on Signal Purple (`#8b5cf6`) background

**Variations:**

| Context | Treatment |
|---------|-----------|
| Dark backgrounds | White "A" on purple square |
| Light backgrounds | Purple "A" on white square with 1px purple border |
| Monochrome dark | White "A" on `#0e0e14` square |
| Monochrome light | Black "A" on white square |
| Tiny contexts (<16px) | Solid purple square only, no letter |

**Clear space:** Minimum clear space = height of the square ÷ 4 on all sides.

**Minimum sizes:** 16px digital, 8mm print.

### Misuse Rules

- Never stretch or distort the proportions
- Never rotate the mark
- Never change the corner radius
- Never place on busy photography without a solid or semi-transparent backing
- Never use colours outside the defined palette
- Never add drop shadows, glows, or bevels

### Co-Branding

When placed alongside another brand's mark, the Ajay Sachdev mark should be equal or smaller in height. Never dominant over a client or employer's brand.

### Font Loading

```html
<link rel="preconnect" href="https://fonts.googleapis.com">
<link rel="preconnect" href="https://fonts.gstatic.com" crossorigin>
<link href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Inter:wght@300;400;500;600&family=Space+Grotesk:wght@400;500;600;700&family=JetBrains+Mono:wght@400;500&display=swap" rel="stylesheet">
```

Google Fonts adds `font-display: swap` by default. Load only the weights listed — no extras.

### Typographic Rules

- Never set body text in all-caps. All-caps is reserved for mono labels.
- Never mix more than two type families in a single card or component.
- Headings always use negative letter-spacing. Body text uses default (0).
- Number displays (metrics, counters) use Space Grotesk 700 with tight tracking.
- Links within body text: accent colour, 1px bottom border at 30% opacity. No underline.

## 8. Iconography

### Style

- **Line icons only.** No filled icons, no duotone, no illustrations pretending to be icons.
- **Stroke weight:** 2px at 24×24 canvas (matches the existing footer SVGs).
- **Corner radius:** Rounded caps and joins.
- **Colour:** Inherits `currentColor` — typically Slate (`--muted`) at rest, Chalk (`--text`) on hover.

### Icon + Label Pairing

- 8px gap between icon and label text
- Icon vertically centred with the text baseline
- Icon size: 16px alongside body text, 20px alongside headings

### Approved Set

The site uses inline SVGs for: LinkedIn, Email, Globe (Uni-Nation), Instagram. Any future icons should match the existing stroke style.

## 9. Graphic Language & Illustration

### Motifs

The brand uses these recurring visual elements:

| Motif | Implementation | Meaning |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Constellation dots | Canvas particles with connecting lines | Connections between disciplines |
| Conic gradient spin | Animated card borders | Energy, active systems |
| Aurora curtains | Background gradient layers | Depth, atmosphere |
| Stroke-to-fill text | SVG hero with clip-path reveal | Building, progress, unveiling |
| Breathing glow | Subtle box-shadow pulse on cards | Living systems, not static |

### Data Visualisation Style

When presenting data (pipeline diagrams, architecture flows):
- Use SVG, not images
- Rounded rectangles for stages (8px radius)
- 1.5px stroke for connectors, colour-coded by stage type
- Animate on scroll with stroke-dasharray drawing
- Mono labels (JetBrains Mono, 8–10px, uppercase with letter-spacing)
- Background: `rgba(255,255,255,0.03)` for stage fills

### Composition Principles

- **Asymmetric.** Content sits left-weighted on desktop. Not centred (except footer CTA).
- **Generous whitespace.** 140px section padding. Let content breathe.
- **Layered depth.** Background → aurora → content → cards → spotlights. Multiple visual planes.
- **Progressive disclosure.** Content reveals on scroll. Nothing dumps on load.

## 11. Motion & Animation

### Philosophy

Motion reveals structure and intelligence. Nothing decorates. Every animation answers the question: "Why is this moving?"

### Easing Curves

| Name | CSS / GSAP | Usage |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Smooth out | `cubic-bezier(0.16, 1, 0.3, 1)` / `power3.out` | Scroll reveals, entrance animations |
| Sharp in-out | `power4.inOut` | Preloader, panel wipes, major transitions |
| Elastic snap | `elastic.out(1, 0.5)` | Magnetic button release, tilt reset |
| Linear | `ease: 'none'` | Scroll-scrubbed animations only |

**Never use:** `ease-in` alone (things shouldn't accelerate into view), `bounce`, `back` with overshoot > 1.

### Duration Scale

| Name | Duration | Usage |
|------|----------|-------|
| Instant | 0.01s | Visibility toggles |
| Micro | 0.15–0.25s | Opacity fades, small state changes |
| Short | 0.3–0.4s | Hover transitions, cursor ring lag |
| Medium | 0.6–0.9s | Scroll reveals, card entrances |
| Long | 1.4–2.0s | Counter animations, hero reveal, SVG fill |

### Animation Vocabulary (On-Brand)

| Effect | On-brand | Details |
|--------|----------|---------|
| Fade up | Yes | `opacity: 0 → 1, y: 40 → 0`. Standard entrance. |
| Scale in | Yes | Subtle, max `0.95 → 1`. Never from 0. |
| Stagger | Yes | 0.06–0.1s between siblings. Creates rhythm. |
| Scroll scrub | Yes | Text lighting, progress bar, pipeline drawing. |
| Tilt on hover | Yes | Max 8° rotation. Perspective 1200px. |
| Glow on hover | Yes | Box-shadow or radial gradient. Subtle. |
| Magnetic pull | Yes | Buttons pull toward cursor, snap back elastic. |
| Stroke draw | Yes | SVG dasharray animation. For pipeline diagrams. |
| Parallax layers | Yes | Mouse-reactive. Max 3% of cursor offset. |

### Animation Vocabulary (Off-Brand — Never Use)

| Effect | Why not |
|--------|---------|
| Bounce | Playful, not sharp |
| Spin/rotate | Gratuitous unless it's a loading indicator |
| Shake/wobble | Error-state only, and even then prefer a colour change |
| Slide from right | Content enters from below or fades in. Never from the side (except horizontal scroll). |
| Scale from 0 | Things don't pop into existence. They emerge. |
| Typewriter effect | Overused. The scroll-scrub text lighting is the branded alternative. |

### Stagger Pattern

When revealing a group of elements:
```
stagger: { amount: 0.4–0.6, from: 'start' }
```
Always top-to-bottom or left-to-right. Never random. Never from centre.

### Scroll Behaviour

- Smooth scroll via Lenis (`duration: 1.2`)
- Scroll reveals trigger at `start: 'top 80–85%'`
- Scroll-scrub effects use `scrub: 1` (tight tracking)
- Horizontal scroll uses pinning with `scrub: 1`, not snap
- All scroll triggers fire `once: true` for reveals (don't replay)

### Reduced Motion

When `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce` is active:
- All CSS animations set to `0.01ms` duration
- All GSAP ScrollTriggers killed
- All content set to `visibility: visible, opacity: 1`
- Aurora, particles, canvas, and scroll arrow hidden
- Pipeline diagrams show fully drawn (no animation)
- The site is fully usable and readable without any motion

## 12. Layout System

### Grid

- **Container:** `max-width: 1200px`, centred with `padding: 0 24px`
- **Columns:** No formal grid. Content is single-column with occasional 2-column for about section.
- **Breakpoints:**
  - Desktop: >768px (full animations, horizontal scroll pinned)
  - Mobile: ≤768px (stacked layout, native horizontal scroll, reduced card sizes)
  - Small mobile: ≤600px (compact hero numbers, wrapped layouts)

### Spacing Scale

| Token | Value | Usage |
|-------|-------|-------|
| xs | 6px | Tag gaps, tight element spacing |
| sm | 12px | Button icon gap, compact margins |
| md | 24px | Card grid gaps, about-grid gaps |
| lg | 48px | Hero number gaps, section content margins |
| xl | 72px | Section header to content |
| 2xl | 140px | Section vertical padding |

### Elevation

| Level | Treatment | Usage |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| Ground | Void background | Page surface |
| Card | Surface background + 1px border | Project cards, about groups, capability cards |
| Hover | Surface + radial gradient spotlight | Card hover states |
| Glow | Surface + animated conic-gradient border | Card hover borders |
| Modal | Surface + stronger border + shadow | Future modals/overlays |

### Component Patterns

**Buttons:**
- Primary (filled): Purple background, white text, pill shape (60px radius), glow on hover
- Secondary (outline): Transparent, muted text, 1px border, text brightens on hover
- Footer link: 1px border, icon + text, lifts 2px on hover

**Cards:**
- Standard: 20px radius, 48px padding (28px mobile), animated border on hover, spotlight radial gradient
- Featured: Full width, 64px padding (32px mobile), larger title, more prominent
- Capability: 420px wide (flex, horizontal scroll), 40px padding, numbered

**Tags:**
- Mono font, 0.68rem, 4px 10px padding, 6px radius, subtle border + background

**Status badges:**
- Pill shape, icon dot + text, colour-coded (green=live, amber=dev)

## 13. Brand Architecture

### Model: Endorsed Architecture

The Ajay Sachdev personal brand is the **master brand**. Each project is a sub-brand that carries its own visual identity (accent colour, mockup style) but inherits the core system.

```
Ajay Sachdev (master brand)
├── ajaysachdev.com (personal site)
├── Ajay Brain (purple accent)
├── Trading Bot (green accent)
├── Recruitment Pipeline Engine (blue accent)
├── Uni-Nation.in (amber accent — family business)
├── Infinite Ink (future — needs accent assignment)
└── [future ventures]
```

### What Sub-Brands Inherit (Non-Negotiable)

- Typography system (Space Grotesk + Inter + JetBrains Mono)
- Spacing scale and container widths
- Void/Surface background colours
- Animation easing curves and philosophy
- Voice and tone principles
- Employment sensitivity rules

### What Sub-Brands Can Own

- Accent colour (from the approved palette)
- Mockup/visual style specific to their domain
- Tagline or positioning line specific to the project
- Domain name and favicon variation (using their accent colour)

### Sub-Brand Naming

- Projects are named directly: "Ajay Brain", "Trading Bot"
- No "Ajay Sachdev's Trading Bot" — the site context establishes ownership
- Uni-Nation stands alone as a separate family business brand
- Future ventures: short, lowercase, no hyphens preferred
