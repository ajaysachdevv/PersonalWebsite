Trading Bot

I kept making emotional trades. Read some bad news, panic-sold. Saw a dip, bought too early. The pattern was obvious: I was the weakest link. So I built a system that removes me from the equation. Four agents run every morning at 10:35. One scans 20 FTSE 100 stocks. One checks the technicals. One rates the setup. One audits the risk and decides if the trade goes through. Currently paper trading.

SaaS ProductPrototype

WhatsApp Smart

2.5 billion people use WhatsApp every day. Gmail got smart replies in 2017. WhatsApp still has nothing — no scheduling worth using, no AI, no way to catch up on a hundred messages in a group chat. Every productivity tool out there targets businesses. Nobody's building for the people actually using it. So I'm building a Chrome extension that adds the missing layer. Schedule messages, get AI reply suggestions, adjust your tone, summarise group chats, set follow-up reminders. Everything runs in the browser — messages never leave your device.

02 / About

I grew up in a Sindhi family in Mumbai. My dad ran a textile business, so I learned how to run a business before anyone taught me what a spreadsheet was. Studied Financial Markets at Jai Hind College, one of Mumbai's top commerce colleges. Got my Chartered Wealth Management certification, then went back to the family business and pushed us into garment manufacturing. Decided to move out of my comfort zone and come to the UK (don't ask me why ;)) for an MSc in Finance at Loughborough University. Joined a B2B SaaS scale-up as an intern and ended up running most of it: management accounts, VAT returns, payroll, compliance, onboarding, legals, ops, finance, cross-entity management across three countries. Now CIMA part-qualified, aiming to finish the strategic level mid-2026. Built financial models behind multiple funding rounds, picked up SQL for data analysis, made over fifty Power BI dashboards pulling from GA4, Firebase, and product databases, drafted client, supplier, and employee contracts and NDAs without a law degree. Somewhere along the way I picked up Python through vibe-coding, started building AI systems, and realised the problems I like best are the ones that sit between departments. The messy stuff nobody owns.
