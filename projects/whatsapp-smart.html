2.5 billion people use WhatsApp every day. Zero productivity tools. Gmail got smart replies in 2017. WhatsApp added basic single-message scheduling in February 2026 -- buried in the UI. Still no AI, no way to catch up on a hundred group messages, no follow-up reminders. Nobody's building for the people who just use WhatsApp to talk to friends, family, and colleagues. 01 / The Problem

The missing layer

I kept running into the same friction. Want to schedule a birthday message for midnight? Can't. Need to catch up on a family group chat with 120 unread messages? Scroll through every single one. Want quick reply suggestions when you're juggling ten conversations? Nothing.

The gap is bigger than scheduling. There's no personal productivity layer at all. WhatsApp's own scheduling, added in early 2026, is single-message only with no recurring option. Every third-party tool in this space targets businesses running customer support through the WhatsApp Business API. Personal users got left behind. That's the gap I'm filling.

02 / How It Works

Three-layer architecture

You open WhatsApp Web and the extension is already there -- a small toolbar next to the compose box. Type a message and you'll see AI suggestions, a tone adjuster, and a schedule button. Behind it, everything runs locally in the browser.

A content script injects the UI via shadow DOM so it never clashes with WhatsApp's own styles. A background service worker handles scheduling through Chrome Alarms -- messages go out even if the tab isn't focused. Message access comes from the WPP library (an open-source WhatsApp Web API), not brittle DOM scraping. No server required for core features. No sign-up.

03 / Features

What it does

01

Smart Scheduling

Set a message to go out at a specific time, then add a recurrence rule -- daily, weekly on specific days, monthly. Per-contact office hours mean messages won't fire at 3am; they defer to the next open window. Batch mode sends to up to 50 contacts with staggered delivery (3-10 second gaps between each) so it doesn't look automated.

02

AI Reply Suggestions

Reads the last 15 messages in a conversation and suggests three replies. Claude Haiku 4.5 by default -- latency matters more than depth here. It also builds a voice profile from your sent messages (average length, emoji habits, punctuation, formality) so the suggestions sound like you, not like a chatbot.

03

Tone Adjuster

Five modes: formal, casual, friendly, professional, funny. You draft something quickly and one click rewrites it in the tone you pick. I built this because I kept switching between a group chat with friends and a thread with my manager, and the register was always wrong.

04

Group Chat Summary

Pulls the last 30 messages from a group chat and summarises them -- what was discussed, any decisions, and who owes what. There's a separate "Extract Decisions" mode that returns structured data with assignees and deadlines. Output goes to a notification, a DM to yourself, or back into the group.

05

Undo Send

Every message gets held for a few seconds (you pick how many, 1-10) with a countdown bar. Hit cancel and it never sends. Under the hood it intercepts the send before WhatsApp sees it -- simple, but WhatsApp still doesn't have anything like it.

06

Follow-Up Reminders

Flag any message for follow-up with a timestamp. When it's time, you get a Chrome notification with a link straight to the chat. Group admins can also set keyword alerts -- words like "urgent" or "deadline" trigger a notification the moment they appear.

07

Privacy-First

Everything runs locally. Scheduled messages, voice profiles, office hours, reminders -- all Chrome Storage on your device. The only thing that leaves the browser is the prompt sent to Claude's API when you use an AI feature. You bring your own API key, or use the free tier (10 AI actions a month, proxied through the server). No account needed.
